About Us

Today one of the foremost threats on the Internet is Malicious software, often denoted as malware. With the booming development of Internet and software industry, more and more malware variants are designed to perform various malicious activities becoming major threats to security. The are several methods for classifying of new malware from the existing signatures or code. More antivirus software provides defence mechanism against malwares but still zero-day attack is not achieved. Traditional signature-based detection methods cannot detect variants of malware.

In addition, most behaviour-based methods require a secure and isolated environment to perform malware detection, which is vulnerable to be contaminated. In this project, similar to natural language processing, a novel and efficient approach to static malware analysis is performed, which can automatically learn the opcode sequence patterns of malware. We are modelling malware as a language and assess the feasibility of this approach. The word embedding technique is used to learn the feature vector representation of opcode. We perform experiments on the dataset that includes malware and benign files.
